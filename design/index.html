A note to the implementer

The workshop, laid bare.

These are the colours, fonts, and components from which every page of Édiproof is composed. All pages share a single stylesheet — styles.css — so any change here propagates everywhere. The aesthetic is resolutely analogue in spirit: ink on vellum, wax seals, brass numerals, and the occasional editorial margin note.

III.

The type.

Three faces, three jobs. No Inter, no Roboto, no Space Grotesk.

Newsreader — display, italic, 200–700 Google Fonts
Diplomas cast in cryptography
An impression upon the ledger.
IBM Plex Serif — body, 300–600 Google Fonts

Every certificate issued on Édiproof is a soulbound token — non-transferable, hash-sealed, and permanently attested on-chain. No photocopy, no forgery, no doubt. This body face reads well at small sizes and pairs cleanly with the Newsreader italics used in all headings.
